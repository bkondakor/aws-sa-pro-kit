üéØ Decision Framework for Service Selection

1. Identify the Workload Type

Is it compute, storage, database, networking, analytics, ML, or integration? This narrows down the service category.

2. Consider Operational Requirements

Do you need serverless (less management) or full control? This helps choose between managed and self-managed options.

3. Evaluate Performance Needs

What are the latency, throughput, and consistency requirements? This guides instance types, database engines, and storage classes.

4. Analyze Cost Constraints

What's the budget and pricing model preference (pay-per-use vs reserved capacity)? This affects service tier and commitment levels.

5. Review Scalability Requirements

Is auto-scaling needed? What's the expected growth? This determines service capacity and scaling strategies.

6. Check Compliance and Security

What are the data residency, encryption, and compliance requirements? This impacts service selection and configuration.

7. Consider Integration Needs

What other AWS services or third-party systems need to integrate? This affects service compatibility and data transfer methods.
